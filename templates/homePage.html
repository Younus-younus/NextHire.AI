How to Make Your Resume ATS-Compliant

Follow these proven guidelines to ensure your resume passes applicant tracking systems

✓ Use Standard Section Titles

Use conventional section titles like "Professional Experience" and "Education". Avoid creative titles like "My Career in a Nutshell" that confuse ATS.

✓ Use Standard Fonts

Stick to modern fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman. Some decorative fonts convert to special characters that ATS can't read.

✓ Single Column Layout

Use a single-column layout. While modern ATS handle multi-column layouts, many older systems still struggle with them.

✓ No Tables or Graphics

Avoid tables, graphs, charts, or decorative images. Use tabs and alignment instead. Text must be clear and highlightable.

✓ No Scanned Documents

Never upload scanned images or PDFs created from scans. Your resume's text must be selectable and machine-readable.

✓ Use Keywords Strategically

Include relevant skills and keywords from job descriptions. ATS rank resumes based on keyword matches with the job posting.
